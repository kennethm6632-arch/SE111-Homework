Review

Kenneth  |  April 2026

The Amazing Spider-Manmakes Peter Parker feel like an actual teenager. Andrew Garfield plays him as awkward, funny, and likeable rather than perpetually mopey, and his chemistry with Emma Stone as Gwen Stacy gives the film a romantic energy that feels natural. The film takes its time getting Peter into the suit, and that slower burn actually works in its favor.

The action sequences hold up well, especially the web-slinging through Manhattan which still looks great and realistic. The scenes between Peter and his Uncle Ben are very emotional. If they ever make a third movie i'm sure it would do numbers.

Overall, The Amazing Spider-Man was underated when it first released but is being appreciated now more than ever and people are appreciating it's greatness. View on IMDB →

Trivia

  • Andrew Garfield broke his finger during filming but pushed through the rest of the shoot without telling anyone on set.
  • The filmmakers used real spider silk — harvested from thousands of golden orb-weaver spiders — to study how webbing realistically behaves in motion.
  • Emma Stone originally auditioned for the role of Mary Jane Watson before being recast as Gwen Stacy.
  • Director Marc Webb used long lenses and natural lighting for the high school scenes to give them a documentary-like feel distinct from earlier Spider-Man films.
